Solomon Kin LLC Unveils Autonomous Waze Sub-Surface Propulsion Track Concept

PHILADELPHIA, PA — Solomon Kin LLC announced today the technical framework mapping of its sub-surface high-speed hydro-propulsion transport loop corridor, designed to alleviate commercial vehicle miles traveled along the crowded landside corridors of the Mid-Atlantic. Sited strategically within navigable waters along the federal M-95 Marine Highway Route, the low-friction fluidic capsule system creates an entirely isolated, weather-resistant supply chain rail alternative[cite: 1]. System trials using un-crewed automated bots are scheduled to execute at active port terminal environments[cite: 1].

Released: June 2026  |  Category: Hardware Prototyping

Development Commenced on Buoyant Autonomous Robotic Container (ARC) Prototype

PHILADELPHIA, PA — Addressing a century-old stagnancy in intermodal shipping unit methodologies, the Terra Automated Logistics division has finalized technical design specifications for the Autonomous Robotic Container (ARC)[cite: 1]. Sited as a specialized equipment acquisition, the unit introduces multi-layered safety mechanisms including hydrostatic pressure immersion sensing, automated carbon dioxide ($CO_2$) floatation bladders, and low-Earth-orbit satellite tracking arrays to completely eliminate open-water cargo casualties[cite: 1]. The container utilizes universal machine-to-machine open protocols, allowing cross-platform robotics to autonomously execute secure docking operations[cite: 1].
